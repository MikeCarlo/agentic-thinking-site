## Summary

Mike is joined by Carl from Replit and Chris from Microsoft to break down the new Replit + Fabric Apps (Rayfin) partnership announced at Build. They position Replit as an agentic app-building environment that lowers the barrier for non-technical and semi-technical teams, while Fabric Apps provides first-party enterprise integration with Fabric data and security. In a live demo, they generate a dashboard from a semantic model link, inspect generated DAX/query work, and publish directly into a Fabric workspace. The conversation also covers SQL/auth integration, multi-semantic-model support, tenant-scoped data boundaries, and private-beta rollout details.

## Key Takeaways

- ▸ Replit is presented as an accessible front end for building Fabric-connected apps, especially for users who are not full-time developers.
- ▸ The integration reduces boilerplate by handling common setup pain points like auth and service wiring that usually require deeper Azure/Fabric expertise.
- ▸ In the demo flow, providing a semantic model and intent lets the agent inspect schema, generate queries, and assemble a working dashboard quickly.
- ▸ The published app lands as a Fabric item with managed links and sign-in behavior aligned to Fabric’s first-party hosting model.
- ▸ The stack supports richer app patterns beyond reporting, including SQL-backed write scenarios and combined analytics/application experiences.
- ▸ Rollout status is private beta (enterprise-focused first), with broader availability expected later.
