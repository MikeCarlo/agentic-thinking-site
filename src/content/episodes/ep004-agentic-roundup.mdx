## Summary

Mike Carlo and Mathias Thierbach use this episode to survey the latest movement in agentic AI rather than focus on a single demo. They dig into Anthropic's Opus 4.7 release, the cost implications of newer frontier models, and why prompt routing and model selection are becoming core engineering skills. The conversation also covers Claude Design, the Databricks and Lovable partnership, Salesforce Headless 360, Gemma 4, and OpenAI's new pricing tiers. Throughout the episode, they keep returning to the same theme: capacity, cost, and deployment workflows now matter just as much as raw model quality.

## Key Takeaways

- ▸ Opus 4.7 looks strong, but its updated tokenizer and premium multipliers make cost management a first-class concern.
- ▸ Teams will need better prompt routing so high-end models handle reasoning while cheaper or local models execute more routine work.
- ▸ Claude Design, Lovable, and similar tools show how quickly agentic workflows are moving beyond the terminal into full product and UI generation.
- ▸ Headless and API-first product strategies are becoming more important as vendors prepare for agent-driven interfaces.
- ▸ Gemma 4 stands out as a promising open model for efficient local use on edge or personal hardware.
- ▸ Strong CI/CD and mobile-friendly agent workflows make it easier to review, ship, and iterate on AI-generated changes.
